What To Wear

The dress code we are going for is Beach Formal. We know that is not a very helpful description, so we figured we would give some guidelines. Basically, we want people to be comfortable yet still dressed to impress. It is still formal, but there is a lot of wiggle room in terms of colors and styles. Think of what you would wear to a fancy restaurant on a summer day. Also keep in mind the ceremony is outside on the sand and in the sun (fingers crossed!). Here is some more help:

Gals

  • Remember that is going to be an outdoor ceremony on the sand and under the sun, so think about choosing a light-weight look
  • You don't need to wear heals and wedges, in fact it may even be better to wear some comfortable, fun, yet elegant flats considering all that dancing that you're going to be doing
  • Bring a light sweater, it may get a little chilly later on
  • Both knee length and full length dresses would be good
  • This is what he Bride's maids will be wearing
  • The colors of this wedding are the same that this website uses, that is:

Guys

  • Ties and jackets are not necessary, but there's never anything wrong with wearing a tie and jacket! Also, that jacket may help you keep that special someone warm when that cool, evening zephyr starts to blow.
  • A button down shirt should do the trick. Maybe something that has that polished, yet relaxed look.
  • Don't wear jeans. Khakis, suit pants, and linen pants would all do the trick though.
  • Stay away from shorts.
  • Wear some fancier shoes. Boat shoes or dress shoes would be nice, but your toes should be hidden.
  • The wedding will be sunny, so feel free to bring some shades
